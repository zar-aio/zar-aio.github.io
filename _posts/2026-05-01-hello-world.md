Every developer eventually starts a blog. Here's mine.

## Why bother?

Writing is thinking made visible. I spend a lot of time reading other people's blogs and notes — they have saved me hours of debugging, introduced me to new tools, and occasionally changed how I think. Paying that forward seemed like the right thing to do.

This blog is mostly for me: a place to document what I learn, crystallise half-formed ideas, and keep a record of things I want to remember. If it's useful to someone else along the way, even better.

## What to expect

No fixed schedule, no rigid theme. Expect posts about:

- **Software engineering** — patterns, tooling, things that surprised me
- **Open source** — projects I'm working on or following
- **Short notes** — quick tips, TIL entries, and brain dumps

## How the site is built

This site is a static blog built with [Jekyll](https://jekyllrb.com) and hosted for free on [GitHub Pages](https://pages.github.com). Zero cost, no database, fast by default. The design is intentionally minimal — dark background, clean typography, focus on content.
